Country Peach Dumplings

Description

Fresh peaches wrapped in crescent roll dough, drizzled with a caramel-like sauce, and baked until the fruit is tender. They are soft and gooey on the bottom, but crisp on top, and the taste is very similar to peach cobbler. So easy to make, but taste like you labored all day over them.

Ingredients

  • 2 large fresh peaches, halved and pitted
  • 2 (10 ounce) cans refrigerated crescent roll dough
  • 1 cup butter
  • 1 1/2 cups white sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 1 (12 fluid ounce) can or bottle citrus soda (such as Mountain Dew)

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350 degrees F. Grease a 9x13-inch baking dish.
  2. Cut each peach half into 4 equal wedges; set aside.
  3. Seperate crescent roll dough into triangles. Roll each peach wedge in crescent roll dough starting at the smallest end. Pinch to seal and place in the prepared baking dish.
  4. Melt butter in a small saucepan over low heat. Stir sugar and cinnamon into melted butter until combined. Pour over dumplings, followed by soda.
  5. Bake in the preheated oven until golden brown, 35 to 45 minutes.
